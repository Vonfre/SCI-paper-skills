## Abstract

MOCK DRAFT: Stomatal closure is a central drought-response mechanism, yet how guard cells tune the abundance of negative regulators in the abscisic acid (ABA) pathway remains incompletely understood. Here, we identify the Arabidopsis RING-type E3 ligase DRK1 as a guard-cell-enriched, ABA-induced regulator of stomatal closure. Mock drk1 loss-of-function mutants showed reduced drought survival, increased leaf water loss, and impaired ABA-induced stomatal closure, whereas guard-cell-specific DRK1 expression restored these phenotypes. DRK1 physically associated with the PP2C phosphatase ABI1 and promoted ABI1 ubiquitination in vitro and in planta. In mock time-course assays, ABA accelerated ABI1 turnover in wild type but not in drk1 mutants, coinciding with reduced OST1 activation and diminished SLAC1 phosphorylation/current responses. Genetic reduction of ABI1 partially rescued the stomatal and drought phenotypes of drk1, placing DRK1 upstream of ABI1-dependent control of OST1-SLAC1 signaling. These mock results support a model in which ABA-induced DRK1 acts as a proteostatic amplifier that relieves PP2C-mediated repression in guard cells, linking ubiquitin-dependent protein turnover to rapid drought-responsive stomatal regulation.
